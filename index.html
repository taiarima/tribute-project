Fan Deng

Champion of Learning and Literacy

A Brief Biography

Fan Deng was born to a middle class family in Xi'an, China on March 24, 1976. Although his parents were both teachers, he grew up as a typical student, not being particularly passionate towards education. As a student, he said that he hated exams, and some of his teachers remarked that his grades were good, but not outstanding. Indeed, Fan Deng celebrated after graduating from his master's degree program by throwing away all of his textbooks and swearing to his friends that he would never study again.

After gaining attention during his college years as a skillful member of his university's debate team, Fan Deng began a career working as a television host in 2001. He began his career with high levels of confidence, but quickly discovered that none of his television programs gained traction. After trying everything he could think of to resolve the situation, he felt like his back was against a wall. Finally, he turned to books in search of a solution.

One of the first books that Fan Deng turned to was "7 Habits of Highly Effective People" by Stephen Covey. It was a pivotal moment for him as it marked his first foray into self-directed learning and goal-setting, rather than solely studying to meet the expectations of others. This experience ignited a passion for reading and encouraged him to continue his literary journey with the works of Confucius and other notable authors. Fan Deng's dedication to reading during this period proved to be a transformative experience, convincing him of the value of life-long learning and the profound impact that books can have on an individual's personal growth and development

After stepping away from his television career, Fan Deng transitioned to become a lecturer at Beijing Jiaotong University in 2007. He initially focused on delivering lectures on leadership based on his professional experience and insights gleaned from books. Thanks to his talent for succinctly summarizing complex concepts in an engaging manner, he quickly became a popular lecturer. However, after several years, he felt a strong urge to share the valuable lessons he had learned from books on a wider range of topics. Fan Deng recognized that many people struggled to cultivate a consistent reading habit, often finding it difficult to synthesize the information they read, lacking the time to dedicate to reading, or feeling overwhelmed by the abundance of books to choose from.

In 2013, Fan Deng founded the Fan Deng Reading Club as a means of helping people overcome the difficulties of accessing great ideas and information in books. The organization provides a range of resources, but the weekly lectures delivered by Fan Deng have proven to be the most popular. During these 45-60 minute lectures, he not only summarizes the main ideas of a book, but also draws connections between it and other works, provides examples and anecdotes, relates the ideas to modern Chinese society and Chinese philosophy, and suggests practical applications for improving our lives. While Fan Deng initially focused on practical non-fiction and personal development, the topics now span a wide range, including math, science, philosophy, and history. To date, he has delivered over 400 lectures, attracting more than 60 million users to the Fan Deng Reading Club application.

As of April 2023, the Fan Deng Reading Club is still running strong. Fan Deng continues his mission to share knowledge, to try to help more and more people develop a reading habit, and spread recognition of books and authors that may go unnoticed.
