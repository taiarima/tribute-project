Fan Deng

Champion of Learning and Literacy

A Brief Biography

Fan Deng was born to a middle class family in Xi'an, China on March 24, 1976. Although his parents were both teachers, he grew up as a typical student, not being particularly passionate towards education. As a student, he said that he hated exams, and some of his teachers remarked that his grades were good, but not outstanding. Indeed, Fan Deng celebrated after graduating from his master's degree program by throwing away all of his textbooks and swearing to his friends that he would never study again.

After gaining attention during his college years as a skillful member of his university's debate team, Fan Deng began a career working as a television host in 2001. He began his career with high levels of confidence, but quickly discovered that none of his television programs gained traction. After trying everything he could think of to resolve the situation, he felt like his back was against a wall. Finally, he turned to books in search of a solution.

One of the first books which Fan Deng focused on was "7 Habits of Highly Effective People" by Stephen Covey. This was perhaps his first experience of studying with books to meet his own personal goals, rather than just studying to meet the expectations of others. Through this experience he discovered the joy of reading and learning through books, and eagerly continued his reading journey with the works of Confucius and other significant authors.

After giving up his career in television, Fan Deng became a lecturer at Beijing Jiaotong University in 2007. At the time he began giving lectures on leadership, based both upon his professional experience, and lessons that he learned from books. He became a popular lecturer thanks to his ability to summarize information succinctly in a manner which could hold the audience's attention. After several years of success with his lectures, he felt the desire to share the lessons he learned from books on a variety of topics more widely. He found the information he learned in books invaluable, but discovered that people often were unable to keep up a book habit. He found that people often have the desire to read, but are unable to effectively synthesize the information they read, are not able to make the time to read, or become overwhelmed when they aren't sure which books they shoudl choose.

To surmount these diffulties, Fan Deng founded the Fan Deng Reading Club in 2013. The goal of the organization is to help as many people as possible gain access to great ideas and information in books. Among other resources offered by the organiztaion, the most popular offering is the weekly lecturers delivered by Fan Deng. Each week he gives a lecture, typically 45-60 minutes, summarizing the main ideas of a book. Not only does he clearly explain the content of each book, he ties back the ideas with other books he has previously presented, gives examples and anecdotes, draws relations to modern Chinese society and Chinese philosophy, and offers suggestions of how the information can be applied to improve our lives. While Fan Deng focused on practical non-fiction and personal development in the early years, topics now span everything from math and science to philosophy and history. To date, he has delivered lecturers on over 400 books, and the Fan Deng Reading Club application has accumulated over 60 million users.
